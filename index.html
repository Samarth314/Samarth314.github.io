November 2020

TeenHacks LI 2020 - Second Place - Breakout COVID V2.0

Our team acknowledges that the world has been locked down for the past couple months, missing some of the most pivotal events of the year, and we believed that during these times, many small businesses are worried about when to open, how to stay calm despite hectic circumstances if they shouldn't open, etc. Acknowledging this problem, our team came up with a solution, where we take in a user's (business) location , and based on the location, find out various Covid-19 related characteristics about the area such as total deaths, total infected, total hospitalized among others, use those characteristics for our machine learning model, and let the user know whether or not it is a good idea to be reopening their business right now. If so, what precautions and guidelines should they be abiding by in the store, and if not, what should they be doing at home to keep themselves safe as well as be prepared for when it comes time for their business to open. Breakout COVID can be used as an app and website, which uses Machine Learning model to suggest business companies whether or not they should close down their business or not. This issue started to appear earlier last month, where companies, including Apple, started to close down their stores due to the Coronavirus outbreak. This caused many other companies to debate whether it is too early to or too late to close their business. We realized that based on the data we have about COVID-19, we can find solution to this by training machine learning model to exactly this. The mobile app, which is developed with flutter, and webapp use this machine learning model to tell its customers whether they its too risky or not to close their business. Along with this prediction, the users are also able to look at a bar chart to see the COVID-19 related data with ease and all in one place.

Check out the project @ https://devpost.com/software/breakout-covid-v2

Alameda County Science and Engineering Fair - Bronze in Robots and Intelligent Machines

Alameda County Science and Engineering Fair

Currently, firefighters perform early forest fire detection by doing routine checkups in different areas or use aerial surveillance devices such as drones to view land and see if there is a fire. However, the problems with both of these methods are the lack of efficiency when doing both tasks and the number of resources wasted that could have been used on something more significant. To solve these problems, a couple of Machine Learning algorithms along with an aerial surveillance device being a drone can be implemented. The first Machine Learning algorithm can be a Sequential Neural Network that takes in the month, day, temperature, wind patterns, rain, the FFMC, and DMC index from the FWI system, and the DC and ISI index from the FWI system to find an area that has a high likelihood of a forest fire occurring by outputting a longitude and latitude value. This process occurs on a daily basis. The second Machine Learning algorithm can be an image recognizing algorithm that uses OpenCV to look through each of the 128 x 128-pixel pictures as an input and use a Sequential Neural Network to determine whether or not the picture contains a picture. A drone will be deployed to combine both Machine Learning algorithms. The drone will automatically fly to the longitude and latitude outputted by the first Machine Learning algorithm and use an RTMP connection to have an image to use as an input for the second Machine Learning algorithm to determine if the land contains a fire.
